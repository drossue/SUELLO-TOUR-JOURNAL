Company Journal

Detailed documentation of each company visit and key learnings

Day 1 – November 19, 2025

WorldTech Information Solutions, Inc.

Facilitator: Mrs. Elizabeth

Company Overview

Worldtech Information Solutions, Inc. is a Philippine-based IT consultancy and training company with offices in Cebu, Manila, and an international presence in Australia and Singapore. The company specializes in IT consulting, cybersecurity solutions, and professional training, including authorized certification programs in Microsoft, Cisco, and VMware technologies. Serving banks, government agencies, schools, and large enterprises, Worldtech provides strategic IT guidance and workforce skills development. The company emphasizes cybersecurity, operational technology protection, and capacity building, supported by a team of experienced consultants and certified trainers.

Observations

During our visit, I noticed that the office was compact but efficiently organized, filled with older networking equipment such as routers, switches, and servers, which showed that even small setups could manage complex IT tasks.

Mrs. Elizabeth warmly welcomed us and shared her journey in the IT industry, emphasizing the importance of continuous learning and staying updated in technology trends. She discussed the challenges of cybersecurity in today's digital age, demonstrating how hackers are tracked, how security incidents are managed, and how threats are prevented in real time.

I found it interesting that cybersecurity is not just about technology but also about awareness, teamwork, and proper protocols.

Key Learnings

Company Recognition & Expertise

Worldtech is highly recognized in the field of cybersecurity, with over 10 years of accreditation. They are proud holders of the DICT Cybersecurity Bureau recognition in the Philippines and CREST CAMP certifications from the United Nations. Each employee carries different professional certificates, reflecting their commitment to excellence.

From this visit, I learned that even simple online actions—like using weak passwords or sharing personal information—can expose us to risks. It made me more aware of protecting my personal data, being vigilant online, and understanding that cybersecurity is a shared responsibility.

I also realized that the IT industry requires not only technical skills but also critical thinking, discipline, and constant adaptation to new challenges.

Day 2 – November 20, 2025

CodeChum (Ladechum)

Facilitator: Xavier David Maranga

Company Overview

CodeChum is an educational technology platform based in Cebu, Philippines, designed to make learning programming accessible and engaging for students and teachers. The platform supports various programming languages such as C, C++, Java, Python, and JavaScript. It offers tools for automatic code checking, student performance monitoring, and cheating prevention. Widely used in over 100 schools across the Philippines, CodeChum provides a mobile-friendly interface, a self-study area with AI assistance, and certification opportunities. Founded by Jemar Jude Maranga, the platform aims to empower students and educators by simplifying coding education, enhancing teaching efficiency, and preparing Filipino youth for careers in technology.

Observations

During our visit, I observed the Chief Product Officer sharing his journey with great passion and energy. He spoke about how his childhood experiences shaped his interest in technology and how he discovered his purpose in coding.

His storytelling was simple yet powerful, inspiring everyone in the room. Listening to him made me reflect on my own goals and motivated me to embrace personal challenges. He also shared opportunities for growth in IT, encouraging us to explore, experiment, and learn continuously.

Key Learnings

From this visit, I realized that passion often starts with small experiences and grows through curiosity, consistency, and exploration. The Chief Product Officer emphasized the importance of resilience, courage, and learning from failures, especially in a startup environment.

His story inspired me to take IT seriously, pursue my goals with dedication, and remain open to challenges that help me grow both personally and professionally.

Day 2 – November 20, 2025

Rivan IT Cebu

Facilitator: Mr. Vincent Victor

Company Overview

Rivan IT Cebu is a training center under the network of Rivan School of Technology Inc., established in 1999. It specializes in IT and networking certification courses, including Cisco CCNA/CCNP, Linux, security, and VoIP. The center is ideal for students and professionals looking to build practical skills in networking, IT infrastructure, and IT-hardware integration. While it is focused more on the IT/networking side of electronics, it provides valuable hands-on experience for understanding how devices, servers, and networks operate in real-world environments.

Observations

During our visit, we were guided through basic networking configurations using Cisco devices. The instructor explained each step clearly, showing how to properly connect cables and configure devices to ensure smooth network functionality.

Seeing the actual hardware and observing how different components interact made the learning experience more tangible and engaging. The hands-on setup allowed us to connect theory from the classroom with real-world applications in IT and networking.

Key Learnings

From this visit, I learned how to configure basic network settings and gained insight into how a call center setup operates. The activity taught me that networking requires patience, attention to detail, and logical thinking.

I also realized the importance of practical skills in IT and how foundational knowledge in networking can be a stepping stone toward more advanced technical roles.

Day 3 – November 21, 2025

Mata Technologies, Inc.

Facilitator: Company Team

Company Overview

Mata Technologies Inc. is a homegrown provider of virtual tours for real estate and virtual reality maps for tourist destinations in the Philippines. The company derives its name from the Filipino word "mata," meaning "eyes," reflecting its goal of giving users immersive previews of real estate properties and notable destinations.

Mission

Tourism: To create virtual maps of tourist destinations across the Philippines, highlighting both well-known attractions and hidden gems, and promoting sustainable tourism.

Real Estate: To deliver world-class, interactive, and user-friendly virtual tours of real estate properties.

Vision

Tourism: To positively impact the tourism industry through virtual reality and immersive experiences.

Real Estate: To make virtual tours and maps a standard in the real estate sector in the Philippines.

Observations

The Mata Technologies office was well-organized, with dedicated spaces for editing, design, and project creation. We were shown a room where they showcased their previous projects, highlighting their innovative work.

The staff demonstrated a mix of professionalism and creativity, reflecting their dedication to producing high-quality virtual experiences. Their project "Savoy Cebu" showcased how virtual reality and mapping technology can enhance tourism promotion in an engaging and interactive way.

Key Learnings

From this visit, I learned how virtual maps and immersive experiences can transform the way people explore places. Combining graphic design, technology, and creativity allows companies like Mata Technologies to create impactful solutions for both tourism and real estate.

I also observed how each staff member has a specific role in ensuring that the projects are executed efficiently and effectively. The visit reinforced the importance of teamwork, innovation, and applying technology to improve real-world experiences.

Day 4 – November 22, 2025

City Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Office

City Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Offices (CDRRMOs)

Tourist Assistance and Rescue Section, Immediate Emergency Responders

Company Overview

City Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Offices (CDRRMOs), which stands for Tourist Assistance and Rescue Section, Immediate Emergency Responders, is an emergency response and disaster management unit established by the Provincial Government of Bohol. Its primary mission is to ensure the safety and security of both locals and tourists by providing immediate assistance during emergencies, such as accidents, natural disasters, and medical situations. Operating 24/7 through the emergency hotline 117, the unit is composed of trained responders equipped for rescue, first aid, and disaster operations. The office also collaborates closely with the Philippine National Police (PNP), Bureau of Fire Protection (BFP), and the Department of Tourism (DOT) to promote public safety and maintain a secure environment for visitors in Bohol.

Observations

During our visit, I observed how organized and focused the team was in monitoring the community. Their control room was equipped with multiple CCTV screens, providing 24/7 surveillance coverage of the city.

The staff remained alert at all times, carefully monitoring every area. The workplace had a serious and professional atmosphere, reflecting the team's dedication to keeping Bohol safe.

Key Learnings

From this visit, I learned the importance of continuous monitoring in preventing crimes and responding quickly to emergencies. I also realized how technology, like CCTV systems and emergency communication tools, plays a crucial role in community safety.

The experience highlighted that public safety requires teamwork, preparation, and constant vigilance. Understanding the coordination between different agencies showed me how complex emergency response systems operate to protect communities.
